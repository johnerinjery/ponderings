Human

Hello, Reader. In this page I want to share my Ponderings on Human. Imporant it is to note that I revise these texts if I learn something that changes my prespective drastically. One has to be ready for change at all times after all. However, everything you read here are my thoughts which I have tried to be very critical about.

Standing on a busy roadside, looking at a Woodpecker perched on a close by power line, I found myself thinking about Life.

Oftentimes I have found, when we think deeply about something philosophical, we tend to get stuck by the word we use to describe what we are thinking about. By this I mean, say we are thinking about happiness. What is happiness? When is someone really happy? How do we find happiness? When we think about all this, we oftentimes subconsciously come back to the textbook definition of the word happiness. If the quest was to find out the meaning of the word happiness, it would not be a quest at all – the word is very well defined in a standard lexicon. If this definition was whole and satisfactory, then the question ‘What is Happiness?’ would not have any merit. But none of us thinks that is the case, do we? We can hence conclude that all of us Humans have a subconscious understanding that a definition in words is not sufficient to fully describe an emotion like happiness. A word is a label – but I argue people sometimes tend to think more about the word than the experience when they think about it and try to answer the question of what it is. When this happens, we find ourselves trapped in self-references, and thinking stalls. The same is even truer for Life.

What is Life, is a question that has persisted even with centuries of definitions. If asked, most people list biological checklists and lexical definitions, but I have yet to find someone who is satisfied with the answer they themselves give. The problem of course is similar to what we discussed about happiness above. Something as magnificent and beautifully complex as Life, I feel like it would be degrading to give it a definition.

Hence, I decided to stop trying to answer What is Life. I thought it would be more productive to think, Why am I Living. Again, what is the purpose of life is a question that has stood since the dawn of mankind; However, I believe the reason for this is much simpler. Every generation lives in a world that none of the previous generation lived in. Everything changes. People change, thinking change even the landscape changes. Inevitably even the gist of what the purpose of life is will change over the generations.

A clear distinction between what makes humans different from other animals is a starting point for our discussion. I felt it would be better if I approach the purpose of life starting from this distinction.
